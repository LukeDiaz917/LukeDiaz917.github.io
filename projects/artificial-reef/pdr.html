Preliminary Design Review

Summary of the March 2024 PDR for the modular reef habitat system. The panel endorsed moving the ceramic lattice concept forward with a targeted set of hydrodynamic refinements and validation milestones.

Overview Summary

The review closed with a proceed with revisions decision. Panelists asked the team to finalize the lattice frame geometry before CDR, citing the favorable colonization data and the ease of diver deployment demonstrated in the mock install.

  • Stakeholder needs: in-situ sensors must log dissolved oxygen & turbidity every 10 minutes; divers require sub-45 minute placements in 6 ft surge.
  • Success metrics: ≥18% drag reduction versus the concrete block baseline and survival of 90% of seeded coral fragments at 6 months.
  • Action items: complete CFD sensitivity sweep (due 4/12), lock the sensor pod gasket design (due 4/26), and deliver an updated deployment SOP (due 5/03).

Panel Highlights

  • Commended the modular ballast plates that let divers trim buoyancy underwater without surface support.
  • Requested thermal cycling verification on the ceramic glaze to prove resistance to micro-cracking.
  • Added a mid-June design checkpoint to preview the integrated data logger UI with live tank feeds.

Trade Study Highlights

Evaluated three reef module archetypes against hydrodynamics, deployment effort, biological performance, and lifecycle cost. Weighted score favored the ceramic lattice by 12 points.

  1. Lattice ceramic (selected): score 86/100. Lowest drag profile (Cd 0.72) and 32% higher spat settlement in lab trials. Manufacturing cost $540 per module with reusable molds.
  2. Ribbed concrete block: score 74/100. Durable but required crane deployment and produced only 8% drag reduction relative to existing reefs, triggering diver safety concerns.
  3. Polymer mesh frame: score 63/100. Light and cheap but failed 3 of 5 UV exposure tests and exceeded allowable deflection under 1.2 m surge.

Key Test Data

  • Wave tank run 17B: lattice prototype held position with 18% drag reduction versus control block at 1.0 m/s current.
  • Settlement panel assay recorded 41 juvenile oysters after 30 days on ceramic glaze versus 28 on concrete reference coupons.
  • Thermal cycling (−5 °C to 45 °C) showed no glaze spalling through 120 cycles; full report linked in appendix C.

Risk & Validation Tracker

Top Risks

  • R-07 Structural fatigue: lattice struts may crack after repeated boat wash. Accelerated fatigue testing at the MARIN tank kicks off 4/08 with FEA correlation by 4/22.
  • R-11 Sensor fouling: biofilm on optical DO probe could bias readings. Field trial will compare silicone and copper mesh guards during the April pier deployment.
  • R-14 Permit delay: coastal commission approval may slip due to eelgrass survey backlog. Liaison secured a 5/01 hearing slot; contingency site package is in progress.

Validation Path

  • Complete CFD verification matrix with coarse/fine mesh comparison and publish sensitivity memo by 4/19.
  • Execute pier deployment of two instrumented modules the week of 4/29 to validate installation timeline and data logger uptime.
  • Update validation plan with June dive observations and compare colonization metrics to baseline reef transects.
